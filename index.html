About

I study psychiatric nosology, mental health disparities, scalable interventions, and the intersections between these areas. Currently, I am a PhD candidate in Clinical Psychological Science at Stony Brook University under the mentorship of Dr. Nick Eaton and Dr. Jessica Schleider. Before beginning my graduate studies, I worked in Dr. Dacher Keltner's Berkeley Social Interaction Lab, where I assisted with studies of cultural differences in affective experience, and in Dr. Aaron Fisher's Idiographic Dynamics Lab, where I assisted with studies of individual differences in clinical symptom networks.

More

Currently, I am a clinical intern in the Stony Brook University Consortium Internship Program. I have experience providing individual, couples, family, and group therapy, primarily via CBT and DBT modalities, in both outpatient and inpatient settings.

I also teach at Stony Brook University, including a self-designed upper-division special topics course on models of psychopathology.

As an undergraduate student, I co-founded and directed the Psychology and Cognitive Sciences division of the Undergraduate Lab at Berkeley. I additionally served as the Editor-in-Chief of the Undergraduate Journal of Psychology at Berkeley.

In my spare time, I like to be active, play Animal Crossing and Stardew Valley, and build Lego sets. I am a public transit enthusiast.
