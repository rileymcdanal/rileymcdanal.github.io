Introduction

I am currently a PhD candidate in Clinical Science at Stony Brook University under the mentorship of Dr. Nick Eaton and Dr. Jessica Schleider. I study psychiatric nosology, mental health disparities, accessible interventions, and the intersections between these topics. Before beginning my graduate studies, I worked in Dr. Dacher Keltner's Berkeley Social Interaction Lab, where I studied cultural differences in affective experience, and in Dr. Aaron Fisher's Idiographic Dynamics Lab, where I studied individual differences in clinical symptoms.

About

Besides research:

As an undergraduate student, I co-founded and directed the Psychology and Cognitive Sciences division of the Undergraduate Lab at Berkeley. I additionally served as the Editor-in-Chief of the Undergraduate Journal of Psychology at Berkeley.

As a graduate student, I teach courses at Stony Brook University, including a self-designed upper-division special topics course on psychiatric nosology. I am also currently a clinical extern at the Center for Cognitive and Dialectical Behavior Therapy, where I primarily offer family therapy services.

In my spare time, I like to relax at the park, walk around the city, do strength training, or read a book. I split my time between NY and DC, where my partner is pursuing a PhD in astrophysics.

Solidarity

I unequivocally support people of color, the LGBTQ+ community, disabled people, and those with identities that intersect across these and other communities. I support the reallocation of funding away from police and prisons and toward evidence-based community care, including mental health care, substance use reduction programs, affordable housing, and income redistribution.
